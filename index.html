ROOKIE MAMBA

Kobe Bryant's rookie year in the NBA, during the 1996-1997 season, showcased his prodigious talent as he quickly established himself as a rising star, averaging 7.6 points per game and earning a spot on the NBA All-Rookie Second Team.

PRIME MAMBA

In his prime years, particularly during the mid-2000s, Kobe Bryant was an electrifying force in the NBA, known for his scoring prowess and competitive spirit, winning multiple NBA championships and earning numerous All-Star and All-NBA selections.

MAMBA OUT

Kobe Bryant's retirement year, the 2015-2016 NBA season, was a bittersweet farewell tour as he showcased his enduring skills and received a heartfelt farewell from fans and fellow players, culminating in a remarkable 60-point performance in his final game.

June 26, 1996

NBA DRAFT

Kobe Bryant was drafted in the 1996 NBA Draft directly from high school. He was selected 13th overall by the Charlotte Hornets but was promptly traded to the Los Angeles Lakers in exchange for center Vlade Divac.

1997

SLAM DUNK Champion

In 1997, Kobe Bryant participated in the 1997 All-Star weekend and emerged as the winner of the slam dunk contest.

1999-2002

NBA Champion 3PEAT

From 1999 to 2002, the Los Angeles Lakers won the NBA championship in a remarkable three-peat. Kobe Bryant and Shaquille O'Neal led the team to these NBA championships.

January 22, 2006

81 point Game

On January 22, 2006, Kobe Bryant delivered one of the most iconic performances in NBA history when he scored an astounding 81 points in a single game. Playing for the Los Angeles Lakers against the Toronto Raptors, Kobe put on a display of incredible scoring prowess.

August 24, 2008

Olympic Gold medal

Kobe Bryant was a key member of the U.S. Men's Basketball Team that competed in the 2008 Beijing Olympics. The team, often referred to as the "Redeem Team," aimed to restore the United States' dominance in international basketball after some disappointing finishes in previous Olympics. Kobe played a vital role in helping the team achieve their goal, and they won the gold medal by defeating Spain in the final.

June 14, 2009

NBA Champion

Kobe Bryant and the Los Angeles Lakers won the NBA championship in the 2008-2009 season. This championship marked an important milestone in Kobe's career and the Lakers' history. Kobe was a dominant force throughout the season and the playoffs, and he played a pivotal role in leading the Lakers to the NBA title.

June 19, 2010

NBA Champion

Kobe Bryant and the Los Angeles Lakers indeed won the NBA championship in the 2009-2010 season. This championship marked Kobe's fifth NBA championship with the Lakers. The Lakers defeated the Boston Celtics in the NBA Finals, winning the series in a thrilling seven-game showdown.

August 12, 2012

Olympic Gold Medal

Kobe Bryant was a part of the U.S. Men's Basketball Team that competed in the 2012 London Olympics. He played a key role in the team's success and helped the United States secure another Olympic gold medal in basketball

November 29, 2015

Announce his retirement

Kobe Bryant announced his retirement from professional basketball in a heartfelt essay titled "Dear Basketball." He made this announcement on November 29, 2015, via The Players' Tribune, an online platform where athletes can share their personal stories.

April 13, 2016

KOBE'S 60 POINT GAME

On April 13, 2016, Kobe Bryant played his final NBA game, and it turned into an unforgettable farewell performance. In that game, Kobe scored an astonishing 60 points against the Utah Jazz.

December 18, 2017

Jersey Retirement

Kobe Bryant's jersey retirement took place on December 18, 2017, in a special ceremony held at the Staples Center in Los Angeles. The Los Angeles Lakers retired two of Kobe's jersey numbers, No. 8 and No. 24, to honor his remarkable career and contributions to the franchise

Kobe Bryant's contract with Nike was a multi-year endorsement deal that began in 2003 and continued until his tragic passing in 2020. This partnership made Kobe one of Nike's most iconic and enduring athlete collaborators. The contract included signature shoe lines, apparel, and various promotional commitments, cementing Kobe's legacy as one of the most influential figures in basketball and sports culture.
Kobe Bryant had a longstanding partnership with Spalding, one of the leading manufacturers of basketballs and sports equipment, where he endorsed and collaborated on the development of basketballs, including the iconic "Spalding Kobe Bryant Basketball" series.
Bryant invested $6 million in BodyArmor in 2013 and received approximately 10% of the company and a seat on the board of directors, the documents state. In 2021, Coca-Cola purchased BodyArmor, which was valued at $8 billion at the time.
